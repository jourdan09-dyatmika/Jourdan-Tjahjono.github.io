History

The 24 Hours of Le Mans is an endurance sports car race that has been held annually near Le Mans, France since 1923. It is one of the most prestigious and famous races in the world, and is considered one of the races that makes up the Triple Crown of motorsport, along with the Monaco Grand Prix and the Indianapolis 500. The race is organized by the Automobile Club de l'Ouest and takes place on the Circuit de la Sarthe, which is made up of closed public roads and dedicated racing track.

This led to innovation in producing reliable and fuel-efficient vehicles, as well as cars with better aerodynamics and stability at high speeds. There are two classes of cars that compete in the race: prototypes and Grand Touring cars. These are further broken down into sub-classes, and teams strive for speed, reliability, and efficiency while avoiding mechanical damage. Competing teams have come from a variety of organizations, including manufacturer-backed teams and amateur teams.
